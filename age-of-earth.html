The Age of the Earth

← Faith & Science

Section 1

IntroductionModerate tension

How old is the earth — and does the Bible tell us?

Modern science dates the universe at ~13.8 billion years and earth at ~4.5 billion years. Traditional Young Earth Creationism dates the earth at 6,000–10,000 years. The gap is nine orders of magnitude. How do Christians account for this?

The CalculationHistorical record

Archbishop Ussher's famous calculation

📐
1650 AD

James Ussher's chronology

Irish Archbishop James Ussher calculated creation occurred in 4004 BC by adding up the ages given in the genealogies of Genesis 5 and 11 (e.g., "when Seth had lived 105 years, he became the father of Enosh," Genesis 5:6), then working forward through datable later history (the reigns of kings, the Persian period, etc.) to anchor the chronology to known historical dates. This was a serious, careful scholarly project for its time, not an arbitrary guess — but it depends entirely on one crucial assumption.

הוֹלִידholidHebrew

"Became the father of" — but can mean "ancestor of," skipping generations

The crucial assumption behind Ussher's chronology is that the genealogies in Genesis 5 and 11 are complete, continuous chains with no gaps. But the Hebrew verb holid ("begat" in KJV) is demonstrably used elsewhere in Scripture to mean "became the ancestor of" across multiple skipped generations, not strictly "became the immediate biological father of." Matthew 1:8 itself says "Joram the father of Uzziah" — yet 1 Chronicles 3:11–12 shows three full generations (kings Ahaziah, Joash, and Amaziah) between them. If Genesis 5 and 11 follow the same common ancient Near Eastern genealogical convention of selective, telescoped listing (naming notable ancestors rather than every single generation), Ussher's specific 4004 BC date loses its foundation entirely — even though the genealogies themselves remain historically meaningful as records of significant ancestors.

Multiple Independent Lines of EvidenceScientific evidence

Why scientists date the earth at 4.5 billion years

☢️

Radiometric dating

Multiple independent radioactive decay systems (uranium-lead, potassium-argon, rubidium-strontium, samarium-neodymium) — each with completely different decay mechanisms and rates, cross-checked against each other — converge on ages in the billions of years for the oldest earth rocks and meteorites. This cross-method convergence is considered particularly strong evidence by geologists.

✨

Starlight and cosmic distances

We observe galaxies and supernova events billions of light-years away, meaning their light has been traveling for billions of years to reach us. A young universe would require God to have created light already mid-transit depicting cosmic events (like supernovas) that, on a young-earth timeline, never actually happened — a scenario some young-earth theologians themselves find theologically uncomfortable.

🧊

Ice cores and tree rings

Antarctic and Greenland ice cores show countable annual layers (like tree rings) extending back several hundred thousand years; dendrochronology (tree-ring dating), cross-matched across overlapping living and dead trees, extends verified records back roughly 14,000 years.

Scholarly Perspectives

What scientists and theologians say

Just as Adam was created as a mature adult rather than an infant, God created the universe with the appearance of age — distant starlight already in transit, rocks already formed with apparent age signatures. This is a coherent theological position, even if it cannot be independently verified by the very scientific methods it says were designed to show apparent (not real) age.

Jason LisleAstrophysicist; Institute for Creation Research; young earth creationist

The genealogies of Genesis 5 and 11 were never intended to function as a tight, gapless chronological yardstick for dating creation. Ancient Near Eastern genealogies routinely served theological and social purposes — establishing legitimacy, connecting key figures — more than providing precise consecutive chronicles, a literary convention well-documented outside the Bible as well.

Gleason ArcherTrinity Evangelical Divinity School; Old Testament scholar; author of Encyclopedia of Bible Difficulties (1982)

The convergence of multiple independent dating methods — radiometric, astronomical, glaciological — on a consistent multi-billion-year age represents one of the strongest, most independently cross-verified conclusions in all of modern science. Treating it as scientifically uncertain or contested misrepresents the actual state of the evidence.

Hugh RossAstrophysicist; Reasons to Believe; old earth creationist

Christian Positions

How Christians have responded to the evidence

Young Earth: Appearance of Age / Flood Geology

God created a mature universe; the Flood reshaped geology

God created with the appearance of age. Noah's global flood catastrophically reshaped geology in ways that mimic billions of years through radically accelerated natural processes. Requires trusting Scripture's plain sense over standard scientific interpretation of physical evidence.

Old Earth: The Bible Does Not Date Creation

Scripture is silent on the earth's age; science can fill that gap

The genealogies are not tight chronological chains. Genesis 1 uses literary, theological language, not scientific dating. God used billions of years of natural process to prepare the world; humanity's special creation and moral nature remain fully intact regardless of timescale.

Ancient Cosmology View

Genesis describes the ancient Near Eastern picture of the cosmos, not ours

Genesis 1 reflects an ancient cosmological framework (a flat earth under a solid dome) describing the functional ordering of a cosmic temple, not material origins in a modern physical sense. Asking whether it agrees with modern cosmology asks the wrong question of the text entirely.
